# CHHARCOP — PRIVACY POLICY

**Last Updated:** March 29, 2026
**Effective Date:** March 29, 2026

---

## 1. INTRODUCTION

This Privacy Policy describes what data Chharcop ("the Software") collects, how it is processed, where it is stored, and what rights data subjects have regarding their personal information. This policy applies to all users of Chharcop and to all individuals whose data may be processed through the Software.

Chharcop is an open-source, locally-run OSINT investigation tool. **It does not transmit data to the Owner, any cloud service, or any third party operated by the Owner.** All data processing occurs on the user's own system under the user's sole control.

---

## 2. DATA CONTROLLER

### 2.1 The Owner Is NOT the Data Controller

Chharith Oeun ("Owner") is the developer and maintainer of Chharcop. The Owner does not collect, receive, access, or control any data that users process through the Software. The Owner has no visibility into what data any user collects, stores, or processes.

### 2.2 The User IS the Data Controller

If you use Chharcop, **you are the data controller** (as defined by GDPR Article 4(7), CCPA, and equivalent privacy laws) for all personal data processed through your use of the Software. You bear sole responsibility for:

- Establishing a lawful basis for processing personal data
- Complying with data protection laws applicable in your jurisdiction and the jurisdiction of data subjects
- Responding to data subject access, deletion, and other rights requests
- Implementing appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Conducting data protection impact assessments where required
- Reporting data breaches to supervisory authorities and affected individuals as required by law

## 3. WHAT DATA CHHARCOP PROCESSES

### 3.1 Data Collected from Public Sources

When a user initiates an investigation, Chharcop may collect the following categories of data from publicly available sources:

| Data Category | Examples | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Domain Registration Data | Registrant name, organization, email, address, phone number, creation/expiration dates | Public WHOIS/RDAP services |
| DNS Records | A, AAAA, MX, TXT, NS, CNAME, SOA records, nameservers | Public DNS queries |
| SSL/TLS Certificate Data | Certificate issuer, subject, validity dates, Subject Alternative Names (SANs), fingerprint | Certificate transparency logs |
| Public Platform Profiles | Usernames, display names, profile bios, avatar URLs, public activity, gaming statistics, trade history | Steam, Discord, Xbox, PSN, Epic Games (public profiles only) |
| Public Posts and Messages | Publicly posted forum messages, comments, reviews, public chat messages | Public forums, social platforms |
| Username Enumeration Results | Presence or absence of a username across platforms | Public profile pages |
| IP and Network Data | IP addresses, geolocation data, ISP information, autonomous system numbers | WHOIS, public IP lookup services |
| Reverse Image Results | URLs where a specific image appears publicly | Reverse image search services |

### 3.2 Data Generated by Chharcop

| Data Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Evidence Reports | PDF, DOCX, or HTML reports summarizing investigation findings |
| Chain of Custody Logs | Timestamped logs documenting when data was collected, from what source, and by whom |
| Cryptographic Hashes | SHA-256 hashes of all collected data to verify evidence integrity |
| Investigation Metadata | Investigation IDs, timestamps, user-configured parameters, query logs |

### 3.3 Data NOT Collected by Chharcop

Chharcop is designed to avoid collecting:

- Private messages, direct messages, or encrypted communications
- Data behind authentication or login walls
- Financial account numbers, credit card numbers, or banking data
- Social Security numbers, government-issued IDs, or tax identification numbers
- Medical, health, biometric, or genetic data
- Passwords, credentials, or authentication tokens of investigated subjects
- Data from compromised, breached, or leaked databases

## 4. HOW DATA IS PROCESSED

### 4.1 Local Processing Only

All data processing occurs **locally** on the user's own computer or server. Chharcop:

- Does NOT transmit collected data to any remote server operated by the Owner
- Does NOT use cloud storage, cloud databases, or cloud analytics
- Does NOT include telemetry, usage analytics, or phone-home functionality
- Stores all data in a local SQLite database on the user's filesystem

### 4.2 Third-Party API Queries

When Chharcop queries third-party services (WHOIS providers, DNS resolvers, platform APIs, Shodan, VirusTotal), those third parties may log your queries according to their own privacy policies. The Owner has no control over third-party data practices. Users should review the privacy policies of:

- ICANN WHOIS/RDAP providers
- Steam (store.steampowered.com/privacy_agreement)
- Discord (discord.com/privacy)
- Microsoft/Xbox (privacy.microsoft.com)
- Sony/PlayStation (playstation.com/legal/privacy-policy)
- Epic Games (epicgames.com/privacypolicy)
- Shodan (shodan.io/privacy)
- VirusTotal (virustotal.com/gui/terms-of-service)
- Any other service configured through Chharcop's integration settings

### 4.3 Report Generation

When a user generates an evidence report, Chharcop compiles collected data into a structured document. The report is saved locally and is NOT automatically transmitted to any third party, including law enforcement. The user decides when and to whom to submit reports.

---

## 5. DATA STORAGE AND SECURITY

### 5.1 Local Storage

All data is stored in a local SQLite database and in the file system of the user's computer. The Owner does not provide or maintain storage infrastructure.

### 5.2 User's Security Responsibility

As the data controller, the user is responsible for:

- Encrypting the local database and report files at rest
- Implementing access controls to prevent unauthorized access to investigation data
- Securing the computer or server on which Chharcop runs
- Using full-disk encryption where appropriate
- Maintaining secure backup procedures
- Implementing secure disposal procedures when data is no longer needed

### 5.3 Evidence Integrity

Chharcop automatically generates SHA-256 cryptographic hashes of collected data and maintains chain-of-custody logs with timestamps. These features support evidence integrity but do not substitute for a comprehensive digital forensics workflow.

## 6. DATA RETENTION

### 6.1 No Centralized Retention

Because the Owner does not collect or store any user data, the Owner has no data retention obligations with respect to Chharcop usage.

### 6.2 User Retention Obligations

Users must establish and follow a data retention schedule appropriate to their jurisdiction and use case. Recommended practices include:

- Retaining investigation data only as long as necessary for the stated investigation purpose
- Deleting investigation data within a reasonable period after the investigation concludes (suggested: 6 months unless ongoing legal proceedings require longer retention)
- Maintaining documented justification for any extended retention periods
- Securely destroying data (not merely deleting files — use secure erasure tools) when retention periods expire

## 7. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

### 7.1 Rights Under GDPR (EU/EEA/UK)

Individuals whose personal data is processed through Chharcop have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation:

- **Right of Access (Article 15):** The right to obtain confirmation of whether their personal data is being processed and a copy of that data
- **Right to Rectification (Article 16):** The right to correct inaccurate personal data
- **Right to Erasure (Article 17):** The right to have personal data deleted, subject to exceptions for legal claims and public interest
- **Right to Restriction (Article 18):** The right to restrict processing under certain circumstances
- **Right to Data Portability (Article 20):** The right to receive personal data in a structured, machine-readable format
- **Right to Object (Article 21):** The right to object to processing based on legitimate interest
- **Right to Lodge a Complaint:** The right to complain to a supervisory authority

### 7.2 Rights Under CCPA/CPRA (California)

California residents have the right to know what personal information is collected, to delete it, to opt out of its sale or sharing, and to non-discrimination for exercising their rights.

### 7.3 Rights Under Other Laws

Data subjects may have additional rights under PIPEDA (Canada), the Australia Privacy Act 1988, the New York SHIELD Act, the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act, and other applicable laws. Users (as data controllers) must honor all applicable rights.

### 7.4 How to Exercise Rights

Because the Owner does not process personal data through Chharcop, data subject requests should be directed to the **user who conducted the investigation**, not to the Owner. If a data subject contacts the Owner regarding data collected by a Chharcop user, the Owner will make reasonable efforts to direct the data subject to the appropriate user (data controller), but the Owner has no obligation or ability to access, modify, or delete data processed by individual users.
