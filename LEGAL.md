## 3. DATA COLLECTION SCOPE AND LIMITATIONS

### 3.1 What Chharcop Collects

Chharcop is designed to access **only publicly available data** — meaning information that is accessible without authentication, login credentials, or the circumvention of any technical access restriction. This includes:

- **WHOIS Records:** Domain registration data available through ICANN-accredited WHOIS/RDAP services
- **DNS Records:** Publicly queryable domain name system records (A, AAAA, MX, TXT, NS, CNAME, SOA)
- **SSL/TLS Certificate Data:** Certificate transparency logs and publicly issued certificate metadata
- **Public Platform Profiles:** User profiles on gaming and social platforms that are configured as publicly visible by the account holder
- **Public Forum Posts:** Publicly accessible messages, comments, and posts

### 3.2 What Chharcop Does NOT Collect

- Private messages, direct messages, or encrypted communications
- Data behind login walls, paywalls, or authentication barriers
- Financial records, banking data, or payment information
- Medical, health, or biometric data
- Data obtained through network interception, packet sniffing, or man-in-the-middle techniques
- Data from data breaches, leaked databases, or compromised systems

### 3.3 Data Minimization

Chharcop follows a data minimization principle: it collects only the data reasonably necessary for fraud investigation purposes. Users are responsible for configuring the tool to avoid excessive or unnecessary data collection, particularly when investigating subjects who may reside in jurisdictions with strict privacy laws.

### 3.4 Data Retention

All data collected by Chharcop is stored **locally** on the user's own system. The Owner does not operate any cloud service, centralized database, or remote data store for Chharcop. Users are solely responsible for:

- Securely storing collected data
- Implementing appropriate retention schedules
- Deleting data when no longer needed for the stated investigation purpose
- Complying with data subject deletion requests under applicable law
